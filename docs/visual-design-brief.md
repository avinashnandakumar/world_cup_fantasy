# Visual Design Brief

## Direction

The dashboard should feel colorful, energetic, and unmistakably soccer-focused while staying simple enough to understand at a glance.

The experience should avoid a crowded admin-dashboard feel. The first screen should tell one clear story: who is leading, what is changing live, and which rosters still have life.

## Visual Ingredients

- Colorful full-page backgrounds inspired by soccer fields, stadium lights, country colors, match posters, and tournament energy.
- Subtle trophy/world tournament visual language without directly copying official World Cup logos or protected marks.
- Country flags used as active UI elements, not just tiny labels.
- Soccer-themed details such as pitch lines, match balls, brackets, score bugs, scarves, and broadcast-style lower thirds.
- Motion used for meaning: rank movement, live match swings, score changes, typing/rotating headlines, and gentle background motion.
- Fluid scrolling with clear section rhythm and less boxed-in panel clutter.

## Simplicity Rules

- Keep one obvious primary focus per viewport.
- Lead with the league race, then live match impact, then selected manager roster.
- Keep scoring audit and rules available but out of the main visual path.
- Prefer large, calm surfaces over many equal-weight cards.
- Use progressive disclosure for detailed roster and ledger data.

## Requested Dynamic Elements

- Rotating or typing headline in the hero/race area.
- Animated rank changes and point swings.
- Flag ribbons, flag chips, or flag trails as navigational/identity elements.
- Smooth scroll transitions between major dashboard moments.
- Subtle soccer background animation that does not distract from scores.
